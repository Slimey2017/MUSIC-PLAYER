WHAT FREQ IS

A player that doesn't care where your music lives.

FREQ started as a single HTML file that played local tracks in a browser tab. It's since grown into a full platform: sign in, publish your own tracks and music videos, follow other artists, build and share playlists, and watch real play counts move in an analytics dashboard — all without leaving the deck.

Under the hood it's one Node/Express server and a Supabase database. No framework, no team — one developer builds every layer, from the database migrations to the CRT scanline on your screen.

FAQ

Questions people actually ask

Is FREQ affiliated with Spotify, YouTube, Apple Music, or the others?

No. FREQ is an independent player that embeds official players and links out to those services. It doesn't rip, download, host, or resell anyone else's audio.

Do I need an account to listen?

No — drop in local files or link a stream and go. An account unlocks publishing your own tracks and videos, playlists, following artists, and the social feed.

Do I need an email address to sign up?

No. A FREQ account only needs a username, display name, and password. Passwords are hashed with PBKDF2-SHA256 and never stored in plain text — but that also means account recovery is limited if you don't have an email on file, so pick a password you'll remember.

What can I upload as an artist?

Your own tracks and music videos (up to 500MB per video, MP4/WebM/MOV), published straight to your artist profile with collaborator credits if you worked with someone.

Does FREQ check copyright on what I upload?

FREQ is a player and community platform, not a licensed distributor. You're responsible for owning or licensing what you publish. FREQ can't pre-screen every upload, but it responds to valid takedown notices and terminates repeat infringers.

How does explicit content work?

Everyone sees Clean content by default. Artists flag releases as Clean, Parental Guidance, or Explicit (18+) at upload, and listeners must be 18+ to enable explicit visibility in their own settings. Sexually explicit audio or video isn't allowed under any flag.

Who moderates the platform?

A report button sits on every post, profile, and release. FREQ has zero tolerance for hate speech, and flagged content goes to an admin review queue.

What data does FREQ actually store about me?

Your account record is just username, display name, and hashed password. Things like your queue, play history, theme, and player settings stay in your browser's local storage and are never sent to FREQ's servers unless a specific synced feature (like cloud playlists) needs them.

Can I delete my account and everything in it?

Yes — you can delete individual uploads, posts, or releases, or your entire account. You can also wipe all locally-stored data (queue, history, favorites, settings) at once from Settings → Clear All Local Data.

Who actually builds FREQ?

One developer — design, backend, database, and frontend, end to end. Read the full Privacy Policy and Terms of Service for exactly how your data is handled.
