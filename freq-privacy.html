Section 01

Overview

This Privacy Policy explains what information FREQ ("the Platform," "we," "us") collects, how it's used, and the choices you have. It should be read alongside our Terms of Service, which governs your use of the Platform more broadly.

Note

FREQ is an independent platform operated by a solo developer. It is not affiliated with YouTube, Spotify, Tidal, SoundCloud, Apple Music, or any other third-party streaming service referenced in this Policy.

FREQ is built around a simple principle: collect what's needed to run the Platform, and nothing more. Creating an account requires an email address, used for account recovery and to verify FREQ Premium purchases. Most of your personal settings — queue, history, favorites, theme — never leave your device. The sections below explain exactly what does.

Section 02

Information We Collect

We collect information in three ways: information you provide directly, information generated by your use of the Platform, and information stored locally on your own device that we never receive.

Account credentials Username, display name, email address, password (hashed) FREQ database (Supabase)

We do not collect government ID, payment card numbers, biometric data, or precise location data. FREQ Premium purchases are processed by Gumroad; FREQ never receives or stores your card details — see Payment & Subscription Data below.

Section 03

Account & Authentication Data

Creating a FREQ account requires a username, an email address, a display name, and a password.

ℹ Email Address Required

A valid email address is required to create a FREQ account. It's used to help you recover your account, to verify FREQ Premium purchases against Gumroad's records, and to send important account or service notices. Accounts created before this requirement was introduced may be prompted to add an email address, including when accessing Premium features. We do not use your account email for marketing without your consent.

Passwords are hashed using PBKDF2-SHA256 before storage. We never store your password in plain text and cannot recover it for you directly, but we can send an account-recovery message to the email on file.

When you sign in, FREQ issues a session token that your browser stores locally to keep you authenticated. This token is sent with API requests that require your identity (publishing tracks, liking, following, reporting, and similar actions) and is not used to track you across other websites.

Section 04

Payment & Subscription Data

FREQ offers an optional paid tier, FREQ Premium. Payments for Premium are handled entirely by Gumroad, an independent third-party payment processor — FREQ never receives, transmits, or stores your card number, billing address, or other payment card details.

Category Examples Where It Lives
Subscription status Whether Premium is active, plan type, renewal/expiration date FREQ database (Supabase)
Purchase-verification email The email address used at Gumroad checkout, if different from your account email FREQ database (Supabase), used only to query Gumroad's API
Billing & card details Card numbers, billing address, transaction history Held solely by Gumroad — never sent to or stored by FREQ

To keep your Premium status current, FREQ periodically queries Gumroad's API using the email address tied to your subscription and stores only the resulting status (active, cancelled, expired, renewal date). We do not have access to your Gumroad account, payment method, or full transaction history — cancellations, refunds, and payment-method changes are all managed directly on Gumroad.

Gumroad's own handling of your payment data is governed by Gumroad's privacy policy and terms, not this one.

Section 05

Content You Upload

If you upload audio files, music videos, cover art, an avatar, or a banner image, that content is stored in our cloud storage provider (Supabase Storage) and associated with your account.

  • Private cloud files (your personal library) are delivered only to you via time-limited signed URLs — they are not publicly accessible.
  • Published tracks and music videos are intentionally public once you publish them, and are streamable by anyone via a public endpoint, consistent with the visibility setting (public, unlisted, or private) you choose at publish time.
  • File metadata we store includes filename, file size, upload date, and — where available — embedded audio metadata such as ID3 tags (title, artist, album art).
  • If you delete a published track or your account, associated files are removed from cloud storage, subject to the retention notes in Section 13.

See our Terms of Service, Section 11, for additional detail on cloud storage limits and availability.

Section 06

Local Device Storage

A large part of FREQ's functionality is powered by your browser's local storage rather than our servers. The following stay entirely on your device and are never transmitted to FREQ:

  • Your current queue, play history, and favorites (logged-out / local-only usage)
  • Theme, accent color, and font scale preferences
  • Player settings — repeat mode, add-to-queue behavior, duplicate handling, equalizer/audio-quality presets, visualizer FFT size
  • Per-track saved playback position, so a track resumes where you left off
  • Star ratings you assign to tracks
  • Your "Keep Server Awake," debug mode, and notification preferences

Because this data lives only in your browser, it does not sync across devices unless the equivalent feature (such as cloud-synced playlists) is tied to your account, and it is permanently lost if you clear your browser data or use the Clear All Local Data control in Settings.

Section 07

Listening & Usage Data

To power features like Community Charts, track analytics, and your own listening history, FREQ records play events when you listen to a track.

  • A play event records the track's source URL, platform (YouTube, Spotify, local file, etc.), title, artist (when available), and — for FREQ-published tracks — the track's internal ID.
  • If you're logged in, the play is associated with your account so it can count toward your personal history and the artist's analytics. If you're logged out, the play is recorded anonymously and contributes only to aggregate counts like Community Charts.
  • Audio and video plays are tracked as separate counters and are never merged into a single number.
  • Artists can see aggregate analytics for their own published tracks (total plays, likes, and release association) — never the listening activity of any individual identified listener.

You can turn off local History Tracking in Settings to stop recording your recently-played list on this device. An optional, separate Analytics (anonymous) toggle governs anonymous usage-pattern data intended to help us improve the Platform; this data is not tied to your identity.

Section 08

Cookies & Tracking Technologies

FREQ does not use third-party advertising cookies and does not sell or share your data with advertisers. The Platform primarily relies on browser local storage (see Section 5) rather than cookies for its own functionality.

  • FREQ does not run cross-site tracking pixels, ad networks, or fingerprinting scripts of its own.
  • Embedded third-party players (YouTube, Spotify, SoundCloud, etc.) may set their own cookies when you stream through them, governed entirely by that platform's own policy — see Section 11.
  • A "Nocookie Mode" is offered for YouTube embeds specifically to reduce cookie usage from that embed where YouTube supports it.
Section 09

How We Use Your Information

We use the information described above to:

  • Create and maintain your account, and authenticate your sessions.
  • Operate core features — publishing, playback, playlists, follows, likes, posts, and Community Charts.
  • Display your public profile, artist page, and published content to other users as intended.
  • Respond to support requests and investigate moderation reports.
  • Enforce our Terms of Service, including detecting abuse, spam, and metric manipulation.
  • Maintain and improve the reliability and performance of the Platform.

We do not use your data to serve targeted advertising, and we do not build advertising profiles of our users.

Section 10

How We Share Information

FREQ does not sell your personal information. We share information only in the following limited circumstances:

  • Public by design: content you choose to make public — published tracks, artist profiles, posts, comments, follower lists — is visible to anyone using the Platform, by definition.
  • Collaborators: if you're tagged as a collaborator on a release, your username and credit role are displayed alongside that release.
  • Service providers: we use Supabase (database and file storage), EmailJS (transactional email delivery for support tickets and moderation notifications), and Gumroad (Premium subscription checkout and billing) to operate the Platform. These providers process data on our behalf and are not authorized to use it for their own purposes, except that Gumroad independently controls payment data as described in Payment & Subscription Data.
  • Moderation & reports: if you submit a report, the report's content (reason, details, and your username) is shared with FREQ's moderation system and, via email, with the FREQ administrator who reviews it.
  • Legal requirements: we may disclose information if required by law, subpoena, or other legal process, or to protect the rights, safety, or property of FREQ, our users, or the public.
  • Business transfers: if FREQ is ever involved in a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, user information may be transferred as part of that transaction, subject to this Policy.
Section 11

Third-Party Platforms

FREQ integrates with and embeds content from third-party platforms including YouTube, YouTube Music, Spotify, Tidal, SoundCloud, Apple Music, Amazon Music, Qobuz, and Deezer.

  • When you stream through one of these embeds, your interaction with that embed (playback, volume, and similar signals sent via that platform's own player API) is subject to that platform's own privacy policy, not this one.
  • FREQ does not control what data these third-party platforms collect from embedded players, and is not responsible for their data practices.
  • We recommend reviewing the privacy policies of YouTube, Spotify, Tidal, SoundCloud, Apple Music, Amazon Music, Qobuz, and Deezer directly if you have questions about their own data collection.
  • FREQ is not affiliated with any of the platforms listed above.
Section 12

Data Storage & Security

Account and platform data is stored in a Supabase-hosted PostgreSQL database. Uploaded files are stored in Supabase Storage and delivered via signed, time-limited URLs rather than public, permanent links.

  • Passwords are hashed with PBKDF2-SHA256 and are never stored or transmitted in plain text.
  • The Platform is hosted on Render. As an independent, solo-operated project, FREQ applies reasonable security practices but cannot guarantee absolute security — no online service can.
  • We ask that you use a unique, strong password and avoid reusing credentials from other services.
⚠ No Guarantee

No method of electronic storage or transmission is 100% secure. While we work to protect your information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security, and you provide information to FREQ at your own risk.

Section 13

Data Retention

We retain account and content data for as long as your account remains active, or as needed to provide the Platform's features.

  • Account deletion: you may permanently delete your account at any time from Settings. This action cannot be undone.
  • Upon deletion, your profile, posts, and associated personal account data are removed from active systems. Uploaded files may be deleted from cloud storage as part of this process.
  • Some information may persist in backups for a limited period, or where retention is required to comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, or enforce our agreements.
  • Aggregate, de-identified data (for example, total play counts that no longer reference your account) may be retained even after account deletion.
  • Local browser data (Section 5) is retained until you clear it yourself or clear your browser's storage — FREQ has no way to remotely delete it for you, since we never receive it.
Section 14

Your Choices & Rights

You have meaningful control over your information on FREQ:

  • Access & export: you can export your local settings at any time from Settings → Data Management.
  • Correction: you can edit your display name, bio, avatar, banner, and other profile fields directly from your account or artist page settings.
  • Deletion: you can delete individual uploads, posts, or releases, or delete your entire account, as described in Section 13.
  • Local data control: you can clear all locally-stored data for FREQ (queue, history, favorites, settings) at any time via the Clear All Local Data button in Settings.
  • Opting out of tracking: you can disable local History Tracking and anonymous Analytics independently in Settings → Notifications & Privacy.
  • Visibility controls: you choose whether each release is public, unlisted, or private at the time you publish it.

If you are located in a jurisdiction that grants additional statutory privacy rights (such as the EU/UK GDPR or US state privacy laws), you may also have rights to request a copy of your data, object to certain processing, or lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority. Contact us using the details in Section 18 to make such a request.

Section 15

Children's Privacy

FREQ requires users to be at least 13 years of age, consistent with our Terms of Service, Section 2. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

  • If we become aware that an account belongs to a user under 13, we will take steps to delete that account and associated data.
  • Users aged 13–17 are restricted to Clean-rated content by default. A Parental Controls system, allowing parents or guardians to manage what their child can access, is in development — see our Terms of Service, Section 7, for current status.
  • Parents or guardians who believe their child has provided personal information to FREQ, or has misrepresented their age, should contact us using the details in Section 18 to request account review or deletion.
⚠ Zero Tolerance — Minors

Content that sexualizes, exploits, or endangers minors in any form is strictly prohibited on FREQ, will result in immediate permanent account termination, and will be referred to appropriate law enforcement authorities.

Section 16

By using FREQ, you understand that your information may be transferred to, stored, and processed in a country that may have different data protection laws than your own.

We take reasonable steps to ensure your information is treated securely and in accordance with this Policy regardless of where it is processed.
